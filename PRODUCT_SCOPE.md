# PRODUCT_SCOPE.md

Current product truth and scope guardrails for Chippi.

## 1) Primary user now

- New solo realtors in the U.S. handling renter/leasing leads.

## 2) Launch wedge

Chippi launch wedge is **not** generic CRM breadth.

Wedge:
1. One intake link
2. Structured qualification
3. Explainable AI-assisted lead scoring
4. Lightweight CRM clarity
5. Faster follow-up with less chaos

## 3) Core product promise

“Help realtors qualify renter and leasing leads faster, with less chaos, more context, and a more professional workflow.”

## 4) Current v1 scope (repo-aligned)

- Auth + account entry
- Onboarding wizard to activation
- Intake link setup and public form
- Structured application ingestion into CRM
- Lead scoring with explainable summary
- CRM workflow views for lead handling (leads/contacts/deals)
- AI assistant for workspace context (provider/config dependent)

## 5) Explicit out-of-scope items (unless instructed)

- Broad “all-in-one CRM” expansion
- Enterprise workflow breadth
- Major automation systems not already present
- Product direction rewrites

## 6) Anti-goals

- Do not drift toward generic CRM dashboards with low activation value.
- Do not prioritize feature count over qualification speed.
- Do not introduce “AI magic” without explainability.

## 7) What success looks like (this phase)

- Fast setup and activation (intake link generated)
- Repeated application submission handling
- Reliable lead context in CRM
- Practical scoring that helps follow-up decisions

## 8) Product principles to prevent drift

1. Protect renter/leasing qualification wedge.
2. Activation > vanity metrics.
3. Setup friction must stay low.
4. AI must be practical and explainable.
5. Preserve calm, modern, product-first UX tone.
